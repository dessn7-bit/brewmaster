      15 IBU Magnum at 60 min, 1 oz Tettnang at 10 min

      Imperial Urkel (50F or 66F)

      1. Anthony Cisneros
        March 28, 2020 at 7:18 pm

        Dude, so I finally got around to brewing a test batch of this recipe. I used 90% Francin Pils from Skagit Valley Malting and 10% Vienna (like you recommended). Because I don’t have a fermentation chamber and I’m going to be using this beer for micheladas, I decided I was just going to let it rip at room temp and see what happens. It fermented for 7 days at 70-77 degrees F. After it was done I transferred it to a keg and tossed it in the keezer. Although I thought this beer was going to taste like garbage I was completely floored to find out that this beer was actually a lot better than expected! I know some people will complain about the 70-77 degree fermentation temp, but honestly I’m not a huge lager guy and I think it came out just fine! Perfect beer for a michelada! Thanks for the recommendation!

        Cheers!
